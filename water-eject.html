The Water Eject Protocol: A Scientific Guide

In this guide: 1. How Sound Ejects Water 2. Step-by-Step Instructions 3. Why Rice is a Myth 4. Salt Water Emergencies

Dropping your phone in water is a heart-stopping moment. While modern iPhones and Androids are "water-resistant" (IP68), their speakers are still open to the environment. When water enters the speaker cavity, it muffles the sound and can eventually lead to hardware corrosion.

How Does Sound Eject Water?

The "Water Eject" function isn't magic—it's physics. By playing a low-frequency sine wave (typically 165Hz), the speaker's diaphragm is forced to vibrate at an extreme amplitude. These rapid pulses create air pressure that physically pushes droplets out of the mesh.

Pro Tip: Surface tension is the enemy. By using a specific frequency, we break the bond between the water droplets and the speaker grill.
