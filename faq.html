SYSTEM FAQ
Is this sound safe for my speakers?

Yes. We use specific sine waves (165Hz) designed to vibrate the speaker diaphragm without exceeding safe amplitude limits. It essentially "shakes" the water off without damaging hardware. It is no different than playing music at maximum volume.

How long should I run the cleaning cycle?

We recommend running "Pro Mode" (Auto Cycle) 2-3 times consecutively. If water is still trapped, position your phone upright in a dry, well-ventilated area and wait 15-30 minutes for the remaining micro-film to evaporate.

Does this work for iPhone and Android?

Absolutely. Because Speaker Cleaner Pro operates via the browser, it works on any device with an internal speaker and a web browser. This includes all modern Apple iPhones, Samsung Galaxy devices, Google Pixels, Xiaomi phones, and even laptops.

What should I do if the sound is still muffled?

Wait 10 minutes for evaporation, then try the cycle again. Gravity is your most important tool here—ensure you keep the speaker grill facing straight down toward the floor while the high-frequency sound plays.

Why can't I hear anything?

Your media volume might be set too low, or your phone might be trying to route audio to a disconnected Bluetooth device. Turn your media volume to maximum using your hardware buttons, and ensure Bluetooth is completely disabled in your settings.

My phone fell in Salt Water (Ocean/Sea). What now?

CRITICAL: Salt creates corrosion instantly. Rinse your phone gently with fresh tap water first to remove the salt, THEN use this tool to eject the fresh water. Do not charge the phone until it is 100% dry (wait at least 5-6 hours).

Can I use this on AirPods, Headphones, or Laptops?

Yes. For AirPods/Earbuds: Hold them with the mesh facing down and run the "Apple 165Hz" mode. For Laptops: Place the laptop open on its side (speaker facing down) and run "Pro Mode."

Will using this tool void my warranty?

No. This tool simply plays standard audio files. It is an acoustic utility that does not modify your operating system, require root access, or alter system files. It is entirely safe and warranty-compliant.

My top speaker (earpiece) is quiet but the bottom is fine?

The top earpiece has a much smaller, finer mesh that traps water easily. Use our "Mode 5 (Air 440Hz)" or the Treble Diagnostic specifically for the top speaker, as higher frequencies penetrate smaller holes better than deep bass.

I hear a "crackling" noise. Is my speaker broken?

Not necessarily. Crackling usually means water or hardened dust is physically vibrating against the membrane. Keep running the sound until the crackling stops. If it persists violently after 24-48 hours of drying, you may have permanent hardware damage (a torn cone) requiring a technician.
